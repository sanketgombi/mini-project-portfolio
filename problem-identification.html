Questionnaire

Structured interview questions designed to understand real-world challenges, workflows, and expectations of stakeholders involved in post-harvest flower sorting and horticulture.

1. Primary Stakeholders ‚Äì Horticulture Farmers

Farm & Crop Information

  1. What main crops do you grow and what is their typical cycle length?
  2. What is your farm‚Äôs horticultural land size (acres/hectares)?
  3. What are your irrigation sources? Do you face water shortages?
  4. How do you manage soil health (manure, chemicals, testing)?
  5. What are your biggest annual issues with pests, diseases, or weather?
  6. Which farming task takes the most time and energy?

Harvest & Post-Harvest Handling

  1. How do you decide the best harvest time for maximum value?
  2. Which post-harvest steps (grading, packing) require the most labor?
  3. What are the top quality standards your buyers demand (size, color, etc.)?
  4. How many hours do you spend on manual sorting/grading per batch?
  5. What is your estimated crop loss (%) between harvest and sale?
  6. What storage methods do you use before selling?

Technology & Economics

  1. What tools or machines do you currently use for post-harvest tasks?
  2. What is your biggest concern about using a machine for delicate produce?
  3. What is the maximum rental/purchase price for a sorting machine?
  4. How severe is labor shortage during peak seasons (scale 1‚Äì5)?
2. Secondary Stakeholders ‚Äì Traders & Buyers

Market Demand & Quality

  1. What quality parameters cause batch rejection or downgrade?
  2. How has demand for certified/high-quality produce changed?
  3. What price premium would you pay for machine-sorted produce?
  4. How often do you re-sort produce after receiving it?
  5. What bottlenecks exist in moving produce from farm to facility?
  6. What causes the most wastage at your facility?
3. Tertiary Stakeholders ‚Äì Govt Officers & Fabricators

Policy & Finance

  1. What government policies focus on the horticulture sector?
  2. What financial products support post-harvest technology?
  3. What institutional hurdles limit farmer access to credit?
  4. Would a machine rental/service model qualify for subsidies?
  5. How does crop insurance cover horticulture risk?
  6. What research exists on non-destructive quality assessment?
  7. How effective is technology transfer from labs to farms?
  8. What data should sorting machines collect for forecasting?
  9. What is the estimated annual post-harvest loss in this region?
  10. What single problem should students prioritize solving?
